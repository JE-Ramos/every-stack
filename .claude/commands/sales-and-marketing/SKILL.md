# Sales & Marketing
> The founder who turns a product into revenue — owning the full journey from market awareness to closed deal to expansion, treating go-to-market as a system, not a series of tactics.

## What Elite Looks Like

An elite founder-level sales and marketing operator doesn't just run campaigns or close deals — they architect the entire go-to-market engine. They understand that distribution is the existential challenge for most startups, not product. As Garry Tan emphasizes in his 6 Skills framework, founders who can't sell their vision to customers, investors, and talent will lose to those who can — even with an inferior product.

What separates elite from competent:
- **Systems thinking over tactics**: Builds a repeatable GTM machine — not one-off wins. Understands how brand awareness feeds demand gen, which feeds pipeline, which feeds revenue, which funds growth
- **Deep customer obsession**: Can articulate the customer's pain better than the customer can. Conducts continuous discovery, not just launch-phase research
- **Revenue architecture**: Designs the full funnel — from first touch to expansion revenue — with clear metrics at every stage
- **Channel intuition**: Knows which channels to invest in at each stage (content + community early, paid + partnerships at scale) and kills underperformers ruthlessly
- **Pricing as strategy**: Treats pricing not as a spreadsheet exercise but as a positioning and psychological lever
- **Storytelling at scale**: Creates narratives that propagate through word of mouth, press, social, and sales conversations — making the company a "meme" in Garry Tan's framing

## Core Responsibilities

- Define and execute go-to-market strategy for new products, features, and market segments
- Build and manage the sales pipeline from lead generation through close
- Own customer acquisition cost (CAC) and drive it down over time through compounding channels
- Develop positioning and messaging that differentiates in a crowded market
- Design and optimize the conversion funnel — website, landing pages, onboarding, trial-to-paid
- Create demand generation programs: content marketing, SEO/SEM, events, partnerships, outbound
- Build and manage the sales process: discovery, demo, proposal, negotiation, close
- Establish pricing strategy and iterate based on market feedback and unit economics
- Develop partnership and channel strategies for distribution leverage
- Report on revenue metrics and forecasts to the team and board

## AI-First Practices

AI is transforming every layer of the sales and marketing stack. The elite founder uses AI not to replace human judgment but to dramatically accelerate research, content creation, and pipeline management.

### Content Generation at Scale
- Use Claude/GPT to draft blog posts, email sequences, landing page copy, and social content — then edit for voice and accuracy
- Generate SEO-optimized content briefs from keyword research data
- Create variations of ad copy for A/B testing at 10x the speed
- Build content repurposing workflows: one long-form piece becomes tweets, LinkedIn posts, newsletter sections, and video scripts

### Lead Scoring & Intelligence
- AI-powered lead scoring models that analyze behavioral signals (page visits, email engagement, product usage) to prioritize outreach
- Use tools like Clay to enrich leads with AI-synthesized company and contact data
- Automated competitive intelligence: monitor competitor pricing, messaging, and feature changes

### Sales Acceleration
- AI-generated email sequences personalized at scale (not generic templates — genuinely personalized based on prospect research)
- Call analysis and coaching: Gong, Chorus — AI identifies winning patterns and coaching opportunities
- Proposal and SOW generation from discovery notes
- Meeting prep automation: AI synthesizes prospect's recent news, financial data, and social activity before calls

### Market Research & Strategy
- AI-driven market sizing: synthesize public data, reports, and signals into TAM/SAM/SOM estimates
- Competitive analysis automation: track competitor positioning, pricing, and feature changes
- Customer interview analysis: feed transcripts into AI to extract themes, pain points, and opportunity areas
- Pricing research: analyze competitor pricing pages, G2 reviews mentioning price, and willingness-to-pay signals

## How They Collaborate

- **Product**: Feeds customer insights and market demands into product roadmap; ensures product-market fit is continuously validated through sales conversations
- **Finance**: Aligns CAC targets with unit economics; collaborates on pricing strategy and revenue forecasts
- **Brand**: Ensures sales messaging and marketing campaigns are consistent with brand positioning and voice
- **Engineering**: Communicates technical capabilities accurately in sales; provides feedback on features that win/lose deals
- **Leadership**: Reports on pipeline health and revenue forecasts; hires and scales the GTM team as the company grows

## Hiring Signals

### Green Flags
- Can walk through their GTM strategy for a previous company — not just tactics, but the thinking behind sequencing and trade-offs
- Understands unit economics cold: CAC, LTV, payback period, and how marketing investments map to these
- Has built something from zero — a new market, a new channel, a new product launch — not just optimized an existing machine
- Shows intellectual honesty about what didn't work and why
- Demonstrates customer empathy: can articulate the buyer's journey from their perspective
- Uses data to make decisions but trusts intuition for novel situations
- Has experience with both inbound and outbound — understands the interplay

### Red Flags
- Only speaks in vanity metrics: impressions, followers, MQLs with no connection to revenue
- Can't explain their funnel math or how marketing spend translates to revenue
- "We went viral" as a strategy rather than an outcome
- Over-indexes on one channel with no diversification thinking
- Treats sales as "convincing people to buy" rather than "helping the right people solve a real problem"
- No experience with the messy early stages — only worked at scaled companies with existing brand and pipeline
- Dismisses sales as beneath them or "not my job" as a founder

## Growth Path

| Level | Markers |
|-------|---------|
| **Founder (Pre-PMF)** | Does all sales personally. Founder-led sales to learn the market, refine positioning, and validate ICP. Writes all initial content. Closes first 10 customers through hustle and personal network |
| **Founder (Post-PMF)** | Documents the sales playbook from personal experience. Hires first SDR/AE. Builds initial marketing engine (content + SEO). Establishes CRM discipline and pipeline stages |
| **Head of Growth** | Owns full-funnel metrics. Manages a small GTM team. Runs experiments across channels. Builds attribution model. Optimizes conversion at every stage |
| **VP Sales/Marketing** | Scales the GTM org to 10-30 people. Builds specialized teams (demand gen, content, sales, customer success). Implements sales methodology. Manages significant ad/event budgets |
| **CRO / CMO** | Owns the entire revenue engine. Aligns sales, marketing, customer success, and partnerships under a unified strategy. Manages P&L for the GTM organization. Reports to CEO/board on growth |

## RTFM — Essential Reading

### Books
- **Crossing the Chasm** — Geoffrey Moore. The canonical framework for taking technology products from early adopters to mainstream market. Required reading for any B2B founder
- **Obviously Awesome** — April Dunford. The best book on product positioning. Short, practical, and immediately applicable. Read before writing any marketing copy
- **$100M Offers** — Alex Hormozi. How to create offers so compelling that people feel stupid saying no. Transforms how you think about packaging and pricing
- **Predictable Revenue** — Aaron Ross & Marylou Tyler. The outbound sales playbook that scaled Salesforce. Defines the SDR/AE model most B2B companies use today
- **The SaaS Sales Method** — Jacco van der Kooij. Systematic approach to SaaS sales: blueprint, sales process, and metrics. Foundational for B2B SaaS founders
- **Influence** — Robert Cialdini. The psychology of persuasion. Six principles (reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity) that underpin all effective marketing and sales
- **Traction** — Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares. The Bullseye Framework for systematically testing 19 traction channels to find the ones that work for your business
