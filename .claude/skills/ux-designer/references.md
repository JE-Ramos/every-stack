## RTFM — Essential Reading

### Books
- [Don't Make Me Think](https://sensible.com/dont-make-me-think/) — Steve Krug. The foundational text on web usability — concise, practical, and still relevant decades later. Every designer should read this first
- [The Design of Everyday Things](https://www.nngroup.com/books/design-everyday-things-revised/) — Don Norman. The cognitive science behind good design — affordances, signifiers, mental models, and mappings. Essential theory for every UX practitioner
- [About Face: The Essentials of Interaction Design](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/About+Face%3A+The+Essentials+of+Interaction+Design%2C+4th+Edition-p-9781118766576) — Alan Cooper, Robert Reimann et al. The comprehensive interaction design reference — goal-directed design, personas, patterns, and principles
- [Refactoring UI](https://www.refactoringui.com/) — Adam Wathan & Steve Schoger. Practical visual design tactics for people who build interfaces. Bridges the gap between UX and UI
- [Articulating Design Decisions](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/articulating-design-decisions/9781492079217/) — Tom Greever. How to communicate and defend design decisions to stakeholders — a critical skill that most design books ignore
- [Inclusive Design Patterns](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/inclusive-design-patterns/) — Heydon Pickering. Practical patterns for building accessible interfaces

### Documentation & Guides
- [Laws of UX](https://lawsofux.com/) — Jon Yablonski. Psychology-backed principles for UI/UX design, concisely presented with practical applications
- [Nielsen Norman Group Articles](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/) — The largest collection of evidence-based UX research and guidelines. Start with their top articles list
- [WCAG 2.2 Quick Reference](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/quickref/) — The filterable, practical reference for Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
- [Material Design Guidelines](https://m3.material.io/) — Google's comprehensive design system documentation — useful as a pattern reference even if you're not using Material
- [Apple Human Interface Guidelines](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines) — Platform-specific design patterns for Apple ecosystems

### Articles & Resources
- [Nielsen Norman Group Research Reports](https://www.nngroup.com/reports/) — Deep research on specific UX topics from usability testing, e-commerce, to mobile design
- [Baymard Institute](https://baymard.com/blog) — Evidence-based e-commerce and checkout UX research with specific, actionable guidelines
- [A List Apart](https://alistapart.com/) — Long-running publication on web design, content strategy, and accessibility
- [UX Collective on Medium](https://uxdesign.cc/) — Curated UX articles from practitioners across the industry

### Courses
- [Interaction Design Foundation](https://www.interaction-design.org/) — University-level UX courses covering research, IA, interaction design, and accessibility
- [Google UX Design Certificate](https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/google-ux-design) — Structured introduction to UX methodology on Coursera
