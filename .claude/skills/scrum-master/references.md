## RTFM — Essential Reading

### Books
- **The Scrum Guide** — Ken Schwaber & Jeff Sutherland. The canonical 13-page definition of Scrum. Read it annually. It is shorter than most people think and more nuanced than most people realize. (https://scrumguides.org/)
- **Coaching Agile Teams** — Lyssa Adkins. The essential guide to the coaching stance — when to teach, when to mentor, when to facilitate, and when to coach. Defines the Agile Coaching Competency Framework.
- **The Professional Scrum Master's Handbook** — Stacia Viscardi. Practical, experience-based guide to the daily realities of being a Scrum Master. Covers facilitation, coaching, and organizational challenges.
- **Agile Estimating and Planning** — Mike Cohn. The definitive guide to estimation techniques (story points, planning poker, relative sizing) and release planning. Still relevant despite being published in 2005.
- **Team of Teams** — General Stanley McChrystal. How the US military transformed from hierarchical command-and-control to networked, empowered teams. Directly applicable to agile transformations in large organizations.
- **Actionable Agile Metrics for Predictability** — Daniel Vacanti. The authoritative text on flow metrics: cycle time, throughput, WIP, and probabilistic forecasting. Essential for data-driven Scrum Masters.
- **The Five Dysfunctions of a Team** — Patrick Lencioni. Framework for understanding team dysfunction: absence of trust, fear of conflict, lack of commitment, avoidance of accountability, and inattention to results.

### Docs & Articles
- **Scrum.org Resources** (https://www.scrum.org/resources) — Official Scrum.org learning resources, including the Scrum Guide, evidence-based management framework, and Professional Scrum competencies.
- **Kanban University Resources** (https://kanban.university/) — Resources on the Kanban Method, including the Kanban Guide and training materials.
- **FunRetrospectives** (https://www.funretrospectives.com/) — Comprehensive collection of retrospective activities and formats. Essential for keeping retros fresh.
- **Retromat** (https://retromat.org/) — Interactive retrospective planning tool with dozens of activities organized by phase.
- **Mountain Goat Software Blog** (https://www.mountaingoatsoftware.com/blog) — Mike Cohn's blog on agile planning, estimation, and Scrum practices.
- **Age of Product Blog** (https://age-of-product.com/) — Stefan Wolpers' blog on Scrum Master practices, anti-patterns, and organizational agility.

### Courses & Talks
- **Professional Scrum Master (PSM I, II, III)** — Scrum.org certifications. PSM II and III are genuinely challenging and demonstrate deep understanding.
- **ICAgile Certified Professional in Agile Coaching (ICP-ACC)** — Foundational coaching certification for agile practitioners.
- **Kanban System Design (KSD)** — Kanban University certification. Essential for understanding flow-based approaches.
- **Allen Holub's YouTube Channel** — Sharp, opinionated talks on agile principles versus agile theater.

### OSS Projects / Tools to Study
- **Jira Advanced Roadmaps** — Study how large organizations manage cross-team planning (for better or worse).
- **ActionableAgile Analytics** (https://actionableagile.com/) — Flow metrics visualization tool by Daniel Vacanti. The reference implementation for flow-based agile metrics.
- **Retrium** (https://www.retrium.com/) — Retrospective facilitation tool. Study their facilitation guides even if you don't use the product.
- **Parabol** (https://github.com/ParabolInc/parabol) — Open-source agile meeting facilitation tool. Study their retrospective and standup designs.
