## RTFM — Essential Reading

### Books
- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** by Martin Kleppmann — The definitive guide to databases, distributed systems, and data processing. Non-negotiable reading.
- **API Design Patterns** by JJ Geewax — Practical patterns for designing consistent, usable APIs
- **System Design Interview Vol. 1 & 2** by Alex Xu — Structured approach to designing scalable systems
- **Release It!** by Michael Nygard — Stability patterns and anti-patterns for production systems
- **Database Internals** by Alex Petrov — How databases actually work under the hood

### Documentation & Standards
- [The Twelve-Factor App](https://12factor.net) — Foundational principles for building cloud-native services
- [OpenAPI Specification](https://spec.openapis.org/oas/latest.html) — The standard for REST API contracts
- [gRPC documentation](https://grpc.io/docs/) — Protocol buffers and service definitions
- [PostgreSQL documentation](https://www.postgresql.org/docs/current/) — The best database documentation in existence
- [Redis documentation](https://redis.io/docs/) — Data structures, patterns, and operational guidance

### Articles & Blogs
- Martin Fowler's [Patterns of Distributed Systems](https://martinfowler.com/articles/patterns-of-distributed-systems/) — Foundational distributed systems patterns
- Stripe's engineering blog — Gold standard for API design and reliability engineering
- Brandur Leach's blog ([brandur.org](https://brandur.org)) — Deep dives on PostgreSQL, APIs, and building robust systems
- Julia Evans' blog ([jvns.ca](https://jvns.ca)) — Networking, systems, and debugging made approachable
- Charity Majors' blog and talks — Observability philosophy and practice

### Courses & Projects
- [MIT 6.824 Distributed Systems](https://pdos.csail.mit.edu/6.824/) — The canonical distributed systems course with hands-on labs
- [CMU Database Systems (Andy Pavlo)](https://15445.courses.cs.cmu.edu/) — Deep understanding of database internals
- Contribute to open-source projects: Hono, Fastify, or Prisma to understand framework design; or Kafka/NATS clients to understand messaging patterns
