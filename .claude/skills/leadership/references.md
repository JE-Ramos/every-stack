## RTFM — Essential Reading

### Books
- **The Score Takes Care of Itself** — Bill Walsh. The legendary 49ers coach's leadership philosophy: "Standard of Performance." Defines success as building systems and culture so excellent that winning becomes the natural output. The best leadership book for founders
- **Turn the Ship Around!** — L. David Marquet. How a submarine commander transformed the worst-performing crew into the best by shifting from leader-follower to leader-leader. The blueprint for empowering teams
- **Good to Great** — Jim Collins. Research-based analysis of what separates great companies from good ones. Level 5 Leadership, the Hedgehog Concept, and "First Who, Then What" are foundational frameworks
- **No Rules Rules** — Reed Hastings & Erin Meyer. Netflix's radical culture of freedom and responsibility. Challenges conventional management wisdom on process, approval, and control. Provocative and practical
- **The Effective Executive** — Peter Drucker. Written in 1967, still the most relevant book on executive productivity. Five practices: managing time, choosing what to contribute, mobilizing strength, setting priorities, making effective decisions
- **High Output Management** — Andy Grove. Intel's legendary CEO on the mechanics of management: 1:1s, meetings, decision-making, planning, and organizational leverage. The operating manual for managers
- **The Manager's Path** — Camille Fournier. The modern guide to engineering management, from tech lead to CTO. Essential for technical founders building engineering organizations
- **An Elegant Puzzle** — Will Larson. Systems thinking for engineering management. Organizational design, team sizing, migrations, and the practical mechanics of managing growing engineering orgs

### Articles & Frameworks
- [Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule](http://www.paulgraham.com/makersschedule.html) — Paul Graham. Understanding the two types of schedules and why they conflict
- [Give Away Your Legos](https://review.firstround.com/give-away-your-legos-and-other-commandments-for-scaling-startups/) — First Round Review. Molly Graham on the psychology of scaling and delegation
- [Keith Rabois on How to Operate](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6fQHLK1aIBs) — Lecture at Stanford on operational leadership: editing, delegating, barrel management
