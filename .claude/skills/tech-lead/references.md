## RTFM — Essential Reading

### Books
- **Staff Engineer: Leadership Beyond the Management Track** — Will Larson. The definitive guide to the staff+ IC track. Essential for understanding the tech lead's broader organizational role.
- **The Staff Engineer's Path** — Tanya Reilly. Practical frameworks for big-picture thinking, project execution, and leveling up as a technical leader.
- **An Elegant Puzzle: Systems of Engineering Management** — Will Larson. While aimed at managers, the systems-thinking approach is invaluable for tech leads navigating organizational complexity.
- **A Philosophy of Software Design** — John Ousterhout. Deep treatment of complexity management — the core intellectual challenge of software design.
- **The Pragmatic Programmer** — David Thomas & Andrew Hunt. Timeless advice on craft, pragmatism, and professional development.

### Docs & Articles
- **Google Engineering Practices: Code Review Guidelines** (https://google.github.io/eng-practices/review/) — The gold standard for how to give and receive code reviews.
- **Architecture Decision Records** — Michael Nygard's original blog post and the MADR template (https://adr.github.io/) for structured decision documentation.
- **RFC Process at Oxide Computer** — An excellent example of a real engineering RFC process, publicly documented.
- **The Engineer/Manager Pendulum** — Charity Majors' blog posts on the relationship between technical and management leadership.
- **On Being a Senior Engineer** — John Allspaw's foundational essay on engineering maturity.

### Courses & Talks
- **Distributed Systems Lecture Series** — Martin Kleppmann (University of Cambridge, YouTube). Essential for tech leads working on backend systems.
- **Leading Quality Code Reviews** — Trisha Gee (JetBrains, conference talks). Practical techniques for reviews that teach.
- **StaffEng.com** (https://staffeng.com/) — Will Larson's collection of staff engineer stories and archetypes. Understand the tech lead archetype in context of other staff+ paths.
- **The Manager's Path** — Camille Fournier. Chapter on tech leads is essential reading for anyone in the role or considering it.

### OSS Projects to Study
- **Kubernetes** — Exemplary RFC/KEP process for large-scale technical decision-making.
- **Rust RFC Repository** (https://github.com/rust-lang/rfcs) — One of the best examples of community-driven technical governance.
- **Architecture Decision Records** (https://github.com/joelparkerhenderson/architecture-decision-record) — Comprehensive collection of ADR templates and examples.
