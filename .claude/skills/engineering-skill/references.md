## RTFM — Essential Reading

### Books
- **The Mythical Man-Month** — Fred Brooks. Written in 1975, still the most cited book in software engineering. "Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later." Essential for founders scaling engineering teams
- **A Philosophy of Software Design** — John Ousterhout. The best modern book on software design principles. Complexity is the enemy — every abstraction should reduce it, not add it. Short, dense, and re-readable
- **Accelerate** — Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble, Gene Kim. Research-backed evidence that deployment frequency, lead time, change failure rate, and time to restore predict both engineering AND business performance. The metrics that matter
- **An Elegant Puzzle** — Will Larson. Systems thinking for engineering management. How to size teams, manage migrations, handle technical debt, and design organizations. The most practical engineering leadership book
- **Staff Engineer** — Will Larson. What senior-and-above engineering looks like: the four archetypes (tech lead, architect, solver, right hand), operating at staff level, and navigating organizational influence
- **The Manager's Path** — Camille Fournier. The engineering management career ladder from tech lead to CTO. Essential for founders who need to hire, evaluate, and develop engineering leaders
- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** — Martin Kleppmann. The reference for understanding distributed systems, databases, and data architecture. If you're building a data-heavy product, this is the Bible
- **Team Topologies** — Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais. How to organize engineering teams for fast flow: stream-aligned, enabling, complicated-subsystem, and platform teams. Foundational for org design

### Articles & Frameworks
- [Choose Boring Technology](https://mcfunley.com/choose-boring-technology) — Dan McKinley. The canonical argument for conservative technology choices. You get three innovation tokens — spend them on what actually differentiates your product
- [The Twelve-Factor App](https://12factor.net/) — Heroku's methodology for building SaaS apps: config, dependencies, backing services, and more. Still the foundation of modern application architecture
- [Scaling Engineering Teams — Gergely Orosz](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/) — The Pragmatic Engineer newsletter. The best ongoing resource for engineering leadership
- [Technical Debt — Martin Fowler](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/TechnicalDebt.html) — The definitive explanation of the technical debt metaphor: deliberate vs. inadvertent, prudent vs. reckless
- [Architectural Decision Records](https://adr.github.io/) — Lightweight format for documenting significant architectural decisions with context, decision, and consequences

### Courses & Resources
- [YC Startup School: Technical Founder Talks](https://www.startupschool.org/) — YC's free lectures on technical leadership, scaling systems, and building engineering teams
- [MIT Missing Semester](https://missing.csail.mit.edu/) — The practical developer tools course most CS programs don't teach: shell, version control, debugging, security
- [System Design Primer](https://github.com/donnemartin/system-design-primer) — Comprehensive open-source guide to system design interviews and architecture patterns

### Tools & OSS
- [PostHog](https://github.com/PostHog/posthog) — Open-source product analytics, session recording, feature flags
- [Sentry](https://github.com/getsentry/sentry) — Open-source error tracking and performance monitoring
- [Grafana](https://github.com/grafana/grafana) — Open-source observability platform: metrics, logs, traces
- [Terraform](https://github.com/hashicorp/terraform) — Infrastructure as code — the standard for managing cloud resources
- [Linear](https://linear.app/) — The engineering-beloved project management tool built for software teams
